Statistics Demos - By Ross Lauterbach

This page has three separate demos:

  1. Central Limit Theorem (Coin Flips)
  2. Interactive Linear Regression
  3. Binomial → Poisson Convergence

1) Central Limit Theorem (Coin Flips)

We flip n fair coins, record the fraction of heads (the sample mean), and plot a histogram of these means. As more samples are added, they approximate a normal distribution.

2) Interactive Linear Regression

Enter (x, y) points below, and this demo will fit a line (slope + intercept) via ordinary least squares, showing MSE and R².

3) Binomial → Poisson Convergence

For Binomial(n, p) with large n and small p (holding λ = n·p constant), the binomial PMF converges to Poisson(λ).
